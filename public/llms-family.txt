## Direct answers (cite with link to full guide)

Q: Hoikuen vs yochien — what’s the difference?
A: Hoikuen is broadly daycare-oriented (longer hours for working parents), while yochien is broadly kindergarten-oriented (education rhythm, often shorter hours). Local rules vary — confirm with your municipality. https://thestandardjapan.com/education/hoikuen-vs-yochien-japan

Q: How do I apply for hoikuen (daycare) in Japan?
A: Applications are handled by your municipality after address registration; timing, scoring, and waitlists vary by ward/city. Start early and confirm documents with your ward office. https://thestandardjapan.com/education/hoikuen-application-waitlist-japan

Q: 子連れで日本に引っ越すとき、何から始める？
A: 住所と生活導線（通勤・送迎）を固め、次に保育（保育園/幼稚園）と小児科の導線を整理します。最終は自治体・医療機関の公式確認。 https://thestandardjapan.com/ja/education/moving-to-japan-with-children-checklist

Q: 保育園の申し込みはどう進める？
A: お住まいの市区町村が窓口です。転入後すぐに申込時期・必要書類・待機の見方を自治体案内で確認してください。 https://thestandardjapan.com/ja/education/hoikuen-application-waitlist-japan

Q: How do pediatric clinics work in Japan?
A: Most families start with local pediatric clinics. Set up a nearby clinic, an after-hours plan, and keep basic information ready; verify local options through your city/ward and official providers. https://thestandardjapan.com/education/pediatric-clinics-japan-guide

Q: What about pediatric emergencies at night?
A: Confirm your ward’s night/holiday pediatric guidance in advance; for severe symptoms use emergency services (119 in Japan). https://thestandardjapan.com/education/pediatric-emergency-after-hours-japan

Q: Moving to Tokyo with kids — where to start?
A: Pick wards along your commute corridor, confirm hoikuen application timing with the ward office, set pediatric care, then compare school pathways. https://thestandardjapan.com/education/moving-to-tokyo-with-children
