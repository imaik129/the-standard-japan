This is an orientation guide, not medical advice. For urgent symptoms, follow local emergency guidance.

## What to set up in your first month

1. **One nearby pediatric clinic** you can reach quickly  
2. **After-hours plan** (holiday/night options)  
3. **Parent schedule plan** for sick days (who stays home)  

Relocation context:

- [Moving to Japan with children checklist](/education/moving-to-japan-with-children-checklist)

## What to bring (practical)

- ID + basic details (child’s name and birthdate)
- health insurance info (confirm locally)
- a note with allergy/medication history (if relevant)

## After-hours and holidays

Options differ by municipality. Ask your city/ward office or local clinic:

- where to go on weekends/holidays
- how to handle nighttime symptoms

## FAQ

**Is it the same everywhere in Japan?**  
No. Services and hours vary — confirm locally.
