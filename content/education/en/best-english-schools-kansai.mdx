Kansai — covering Kobe, Osaka, Kyoto, Ashiya, Nishinomiya, and surrounding areas — is Japan's second-largest international education market. For families comparing English-medium schools, the region offers a focused set of established names spread across two main urban corridors.

This guide covers the most frequently compared English-medium schools in Kansai for families relocating, returning, or long-resident in the region.

## Kansai Overview: Kobe vs Osaka Corridors

Families in Kansai typically focus on one of two corridors:

- **Kobe corridor** — Rokko Island, central Kobe, with commuter access from Ashiya and Nishinomiya along the JR and Hankyu lines
- **Osaka corridor** — Northern Osaka (Senri, Toyonaka), central Osaka, and Kyoto-side catchments

Some families commute between corridors; for most, the realistic school choice is determined by where they live.

## English Schools in Kobe

### Canadian Academy

**Canadian Academy (CA)** on Rokko Island is one of the best-known international schools in all of Kansai, not just Kobe. Its profile draws families from across Higashinada, Ashiya, Nishinomiya, and even northern Osaka.

- **Location:** Rokko Island, Kobe
- **Age range:** K–12 (confirm current bands with the school)
- **Curriculum:** International / English-medium; strong university placement record
- **Community:** Large expat and internationally minded Japanese family community; the Rokko Island area has grown around the school's presence
- **Admissions:** Competitive for popular grades; begin research early

Families considering CA often start preschool planning in Rokko Island — see [Preschool Before Canadian Academy](/education/preschool-before-canadian-academy).

Official site: [canacad.ac.jp](https://www.canacad.ac.jp/)

### Marist Brothers International School

**Marist Brothers International School (MBIS)** serves a distinct segment of Kobe's international community with a Catholic-affiliated program.

- Often considered by families seeking a faith-informed international education environment
- English-medium curriculum; compare with CA on structure, commute, and community fit
- Open days and current-parent conversations are the best way to evaluate

See full guide: [Marist Brothers International School Kobe Guide](/education/marist-brothers-kobe-guide)

### St. Michael's International School

**St. Michael's International School** is another well-established Kobe name, particularly for families comparing smaller or more focused environments against CA.

- Worth checking current age range and grade availability
- Located in central Kobe; commute from Ashiya and Nishinomiya is feasible

## English Schools in Osaka

### Osaka YMCA International School

**Osaka YMCA International School** is one of Osaka's longer-standing English-medium schools.

- Centrally located with good urban transport access
- Compare curriculum depth, age range, and class size with Kobe alternatives

### Osaka International School of Kwansei Gakuin (OIS)

**OIS** (Senri, Toyonaka) is a well-regarded option for families in northern Osaka and those comparing Kansai-wide.

- IB-affiliated; strong academic track record
- Located in the Senri Hills area — relevant for families based north of Osaka City

### Other Osaka Options

The Osaka corridor includes several additional schools with different profiles, curricula, and community sizes. Families relocating to Osaka city center, Kyoto, or Nara should compare directly rather than defaulting to Kobe-side schools.

## Preschool and Early English Options

For families with children below primary school age, preschool choice is often as consequential as school choice — both for English exposure and for practical daily life.

**Peter Pan International Preschool** (Rokko Island, Kobe) is one of the most frequently referenced English-speaking preschool options in Kansai for families considering Kobe's international schools.

Canadian Academy's official website notes its longstanding relationship with Peter Pan International Pre-School, which runs an English-language program for 2-year-olds and is located approximately 10 minutes' walk from campus. Peter Pan's own site notes its recent relocation within Rokko Island to **THE ENTENTE**, linked to Canadian Academy's redevelopment.

Peter Pan may be a useful early English environment for families planning a Kobe school pathway, but **attendance does not guarantee admission to any school.** Each school makes its own independent decisions.

## How to Compare Kansai Schools

When evaluating schools across Kansai, prioritize:

1. **Location and commute realism** — Test the actual door-to-door journey at rush hour before committing to a school address
2. **Age range and availability** — Not all schools take all grades; confirm space in your child's year before visiting
3. **Curriculum and university pathways** — IB, American, British, and hybrid programs have different university implications depending on where your family may end up
4. **Language support** — Children entering with limited English need different support at different schools
5. **Community fit** — School tours and parent conversations reveal culture in ways brochures don't
6. **Total cost** — Ask for total annual cost including fees, registration, uniform, bus, and activities

## FAQ

**What is the best English school in Kansai?**
There is no single "best." Canadian Academy is the most frequently cited name in Kobe; OIS is well-regarded in north Osaka. The best school for your family depends on location, curriculum, age, and your child's needs. Compare directly using this guide and official school visits.

**What is the best English school in Kobe?**
Canadian Academy (Rokko Island), Marist Brothers International School, and St. Michael's International School are the main English-medium schools in Kobe. Each has a different profile — compare curriculum, community, location, and total cost.

**Is Canadian Academy the best school in Kansai?**
Canadian Academy is one of the best-known international schools in Kansai, but "best" depends on your child's age, your home location, curriculum preference, and long-term plans. Families in northern Osaka, for example, may find OIS more practical than commuting to Rokko Island.

**Is there an English preschool in Kobe?**
Yes. Peter Pan International Preschool in Rokko Island is one option. See [Best International Preschools in Kobe](/education/best-international-preschools-kobe) for a broader overview.

**How does Kansai compare to Tokyo for international schools?**
Tokyo has a larger and more varied international school market. Kansai's selection is smaller but more navigable — most serious options can be researched and visited in a few weeks. See [Best International Schools in Japan](/education/best-international-schools-japan) for a national overview.

**Can I commute from Ashiya or Nishinomiya to Kobe schools?**
Yes — many families do. See the [Kobe School Commute Guide](/education/kobe-school-commute-guide-rokko-ashiya-nishinomiya) for practical route and timing advice.
