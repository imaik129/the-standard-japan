Relocating to Kansai with children requires more advance planning than any other aspect of the move. School availability, neighborhood choice, and preschool logistics are all connected — and the sequence matters.

This guide walks through what families typically need to plan before and after arriving in Kansai.

## Kansai vs Tokyo: What to Expect

Families relocating from Tokyo — or from abroad with Tokyo as a reference point — should adjust expectations:

| Factor | Tokyo | Kansai |
|--------|-------|--------|
| **International school market** | Largest in Japan | Smaller but navigable |
| **School choice** | 20+ serious options | 5–8 main English-medium options |
| **Community density** | Large, distributed | Concentrated (Rokko Island, Senri area) |
| **Cost of living** | High | Generally lower than Tokyo |
| **Commute culture** | Dense rail network | JR, Hankyu, Hanshin — functional but less granular than Tokyo |
| **English infrastructure** | Extensive | Narrower; Japanese ability helps more |

Kansai is not "Tokyo with fewer options." It has its own community rhythms and geography. Families who go in with Kansai-specific knowledge settle faster.

## The Two Main Family Corridors

Most international families in Kansai orient around one of two corridors:

**Kobe corridor** — Rokko Island / Higashinada / Ashiya / Nishinomiya
- Primary schools: Canadian Academy (Rokko Island), Marist Brothers, St. Michael's
- Community: concentrated expat and internationally minded Japanese families, especially on Rokko Island
- Feel: calmer, more suburban, port-city character

**Osaka corridor** — Senri / Toyonaka / central Osaka / Kyoto-accessible
- Primary schools: Osaka International School (OIS), Osaka YMCA International School, others
- Community: more urban-diffuse; large international business community in central Osaka
- Feel: larger city energy; better for families whose work is Osaka-centered

Families who choose a school first, then a neighborhood, settle more smoothly than those who do it in reverse.

## Kobe Corridor: Schools and Neighborhoods

### Schools

**Canadian Academy** — Rokko Island's anchor institution. K–12, strong university placement, the most recognized name in Kansai for international education. Competitive for popular grades; contact admissions early.

**Marist Brothers International School** — Catholic-affiliated, English-medium, smaller scale than CA. Located in central Kobe. See [Marist Brothers Kobe Guide](/education/marist-brothers-kobe-guide).

**St. Michael's International School** — Established Kobe name, smaller profile. Compare commute from your area.

### Neighborhoods

**Rokko Island** — The most concentrated international family community in Kobe. Close to CA; walkable lifestyle for school-age children. Newer residential development (THE ENTENTE) has expanded options on the island.

**Ashiya** (芦屋) — Upscale residential city between Kobe and Osaka. Many CA families commute by JR. Lower density but well-regarded schools, parks, and residential quality.

**Nishinomiya** (西宮) — Larger than Ashiya; broader housing options and price range. Families commute to Kobe schools; also consider Osaka options depending on work location.

**Higashinada** (東灘区) — The Kobe ward closest to Rokko Island; good commute to CA; residential mix of Japanese and international families.

## Osaka Corridor: Schools and Neighborhoods

### Schools

**Osaka International School of Kwansei Gakuin (OIS)** — Located in Senri/Toyonaka. IB-affiliated, strong academic reputation, established international community.

**Osaka YMCA International School** — Central Osaka location; more accessible by public transport for urban-based families.

### Neighborhoods

**Senri Hills / Toyonaka / Minoh** — Suburban northern Osaka; popular with OIS families. Good parks, lower density.

**Central Osaka** (Umeda, Namba adjacent) — Better for families prioritizing urban amenity over school proximity; commute to schools is longer.

**Kobe–Osaka corridor towns** — Some families live midway (e.g., Amagasaki) and commute to either city; less ideal for families with young children due to commute length.

## Preschool and Early English Options

Families with children below primary school age face an additional layer of planning: **preschool logistics are separate from school logistics**, and the right preschool depends on your neighborhood and school pathway.

For families settling in the **Rokko Island / Kobe** corridor:

**Peter Pan International Preschool** (Rokko Island, THE ENTENTE) is the most commonly referenced English-speaking preschool for Kobe international families. Canadian Academy's official site notes a longstanding relationship with Peter Pan, including a 2-year-old English program approximately 10 minutes from CA's campus.

Peter Pan is particularly relevant for:
- Families living on Rokko Island or within feasible commute
- Families considering Canadian Academy as a future school
- Expat families who want daily English immersion before age 4–5

**Attending Peter Pan does not guarantee admission to Canadian Academy or any school.** Preschool choice and school admission are separate decisions.

## Sequence: What to Plan First

The order matters for Kansai relocation:

1. **Confirm school availability** — Contact your target schools as early as possible; popular grades at CA can fill before you arrive in Japan
2. **Choose neighborhood based on school** — Identify neighborhoods with feasible commutes to your preferred school
3. **Research preschool** — For children under school age, identify preschool options in your chosen area
4. **Visit before committing** — School visits and neighborhood walk-throughs are more informative than research alone
5. **Arrange housing around school zone** — Being 30 minutes vs 10 minutes from school affects daily quality of life significantly

See [Moving to Kobe with Children](/education/moving-to-kobe-international-schools) and [Kobe School Commute Guide](/education/kobe-school-commute-guide-rokko-ashiya-nishinomiya).

## Admin Checklist After Arrival

After arriving in Kansai, families should:

- **Register at the ward office (市区町村)** within 14 days — required for all residents
- **Obtain a boshi techo (母子手帳)** if you have young children — the maternal and child health handbook used throughout Japan
- **Register with a local pediatric clinic (小児科)** before you need one urgently
- **Apply for child allowance (子ども手当)** at the ward office
- **Confirm childcare subsidy eligibility** if using Japanese hoikuen alongside an international preschool
- **Set up National Health Insurance (国民健康保険)** if not covered by employer

See [Moving to Japan with Children Checklist](/education/moving-to-japan-with-children-checklist) and [Ward Registration in Japan](/education/ward-registration-families-japan).

## FAQ

**Which is better for international families — Kobe or Osaka?**
Neither is universally better. Kobe (especially Rokko Island) offers a concentrated international community and calmer suburban environment; Osaka offers more urban scale. Choose based on where you'll work, which school fits your child, and your lifestyle preferences.

**Is Canadian Academy the best option for families moving to Kansai?**
CA is the most recognized name and draws families from across Kansai. But it is not the only option, and for families not living in the Kobe corridor, Osaka schools may be more practical. Confirm availability before planning your move around CA.

**What English preschool options exist near Rokko Island?**
Peter Pan International Preschool (THE ENTENTE, Rokko Island) is the primary English-speaking preschool option in the area. See [Peter Pan Preschool Guide](/education/peter-pan-preschool-kobe-guide) for details.

**How far in advance should I contact international schools in Kansai?**
As early as possible — at least 6–12 months before your planned arrival, especially for popular grades. Some schools have waiting lists for specific year groups.

**Do I need to live on Rokko Island to attend Canadian Academy?**
No — families commute from Ashiya, Nishinomiya, Higashinada, and even parts of Osaka. But living on Rokko Island simplifies daily logistics, especially for primary-age children.

**What are the main family neighborhoods in Kobe?**
Rokko Island (most international-concentrated), Ashiya (upscale, popular with CA families), Nishinomiya (broader range), and Higashinada (good CA commute). See [Rokko Island Family Guide](/education/rokko-island-family-guide).
