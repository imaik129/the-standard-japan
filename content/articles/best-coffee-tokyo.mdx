The best coffee in Tokyo is at Fuglen, Kurasu, Blue Bottle (Aoyama), or a traditional kissaten (Japanese coffee house). Tokyo has one of the world's best coffee scenes — from third-wave roasters to decades-old kissaten. Here's where to go.

## Best Coffee in Tokyo: The Top Picks

**Fuglen** — Norwegian roaster in Yoyogi. Best coffee in Tokyo. Day cafe, night bar. Go.

**Kurasu** — Kyoto roaster with Tokyo outposts. Clean, precise. Kyoto-style pour-over.

**Blue Bottle (Aoyama)** — The Aoyama location is the best. Beautiful space. Good coffee.

**Chatei Hatou** — Shibuya. Traditional kissaten. Syphon coffee. 30+ years. Cash only.

**Cafe de l'ambre** — Ginza. Since 1948. The classic kissaten. Dark, aged beans.

**Fuglen Tokyo** — Yes, twice. It's that good. Yoyogi. Norwegian design, Japanese precision.

**Allpress** — New Zealand roaster. Roppongi, Shibuya. Consistent, good.

**Little Nap Coffee** — Yoyogi. Small, excellent. Single-origin focus.

## Best Coffee in Tokyo: Kissaten vs Third-Wave

**Kissaten** — Traditional Japanese coffee shops. Syphon or drip. Dark roasts. Often decades old. Chatei Hatou, Cafe de l'ambre.

**Third-wave** — Lighter roasts, single-origin, pour-over. Fuglen, Kurasu, Blue Bottle.

**Both** — Tokyo does both well. Try one of each.

## Best Coffee in Tokyo by Neighborhood

**Yoyogi** — Fuglen, Little Nap. The best concentration.

**Shibuya** — Chatei Hatou, Allpress. Mix of old and new.

**Ginza** — Cafe de l'ambre, Blue Bottle. Traditional and modern.

**Nakameguro** — Cafes along the canal. More about the vibe.

**Aoyama** — Blue Bottle, design-focused cafes.

## How Much Does Coffee Cost in Tokyo?

**Kissaten** — ¥500–¥800. Often includes a small sweet.

**Third-wave** — ¥600–¥1,000. Pour-over ¥800–¥1,200.

**Chain (Doutor, Komeda)** — ¥300–¥500. Cheap, decent.

## FAQ

**What is the best coffee in Tokyo?**
Fuglen in Yoyogi. Kurasu, Blue Bottle Aoyama, and Chatei Hatou are also excellent. For traditional, Cafe de l'ambre in Ginza.

**What is a kissaten?**
A traditional Japanese coffee shop. Often decades old. Syphon or drip. Dark roasts. Chatei Hatou and Cafe de l'ambre are the best.

**How much does coffee cost in Tokyo?**
¥500–¥1,000 for a good cup. Kissaten ¥500–¥800. Third-wave ¥600–¥1,000. Chains ¥300–¥500.

**Where is the best coffee in Tokyo?**
Yoyogi (Fuglen, Little Nap), Shibuya (Chatei Hatou), Ginza (Cafe de l'ambre), Aoyama (Blue Bottle).

**Is Tokyo coffee good?**
Yes. One of the best coffee cities in the world. Both traditional kissaten and third-wave are excellent.
