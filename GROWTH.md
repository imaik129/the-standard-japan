## Part 1: Content Strategy — What Makes Articles Go Viral

### The Viral Formula

Content that spreads naturally has three traits:

1. **Strong point of view** — Take a stance. "7-Eleven is the greatest retail invention" beats "Japanese convenience stores are interesting."
2. **Actionable specificity** — Names, addresses, prices, times. "Fuunji in Shinjuku, go at 11am" beats "try tsukemen in Tokyo."
3. **Emotional resonance** — Pride (discovering something first), FOMO (you're missing out), belonging (you're in the know).

### Article Structure That Converts

Every article should follow this skeleton:

```
1. HOOK (first 2 sentences)
   - Bold claim or contrarian take
   - "The first thing you should do in Japan is go to a convenience store."

2. THESIS (paragraph 1–2)
   - What you're arguing and why it matters
   - Set expectations: "This is a fact-based argument."

3. BODY (H2 sections)
   - 3–6 sections with clear H2 headers
   - Each section: specific examples, names, details
   - Use **bold** for key terms and recommendations

4. FAQ (required for AEO)
   - 4–6 questions people actually search
   - Format: ## FAQ, then **Question?** followed by answer
   - Answers should be 1–3 sentences, scannable

5. CTA
   - Newsletter signup, related articles, share
```

|--------|-----------------|---------------|

### What to Write Next (Priority Order)

1. **"Best X in Tokyo"** — High search volume, evergreen. Ramen, sushi, izakaya, coffee, etc.
2. **"How to X in Japan"** — Etiquette, logistics. Onsen, izakaya, convenience store, etc.
3. **Neighborhood guides** — "Shimokitazawa guide," "Nakameguro at night"
4. **Comparisons** — "Kyoto vs Tokyo," "7-Eleven vs Lawson"
5. **Trend pieces** — "Tokyo's underground jazz scene," "Emerging artists in 2025"

## Part 2: SEO Best Practices

### On-Page SEO Checklist (Every Article)

- [ ] **Title**: 50–60 chars, include primary keyword, compelling
- [ ] **Excerpt**: 150–160 chars, keyword in first 50 chars
- [ ] **H2 structure**: 3–6 H2s, keywords in headers where natural
- [ ] **Internal links**: 2–4 links to other articles
- [ ] **External links**: 1–2 to authoritative sources (builds trust)
- [ ] **Images**: Alt text with keywords, descriptive filenames
- [ ] **Tags**: 5–8 relevant tags (used for internal linking)

### Frontmatter Template

```yaml
---
title: "Primary Keyword: Secondary Angle"  # e.g. "Best Ramen in Tokyo: The Definitive Guide"
slug: keyword-rich-descriptive-slug
category: food
excerpt: "First 50 chars with keyword. Rest of 150 chars with benefit."
coverImage: "https://..."
author: "The Standard Japan"
publishedAt: "2025-03-15"
readTime: 8
featured: true
tags: [primary, secondary, location, topic]
---
```

## Part 3: AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)

AEO = optimizing for AI assistants (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews), voice search, and featured snippets.

### What AEO Rewards

1. **Direct answers** — First paragraph should answer the main question
2. **FAQ format** — Q&A format is ideal for extraction
3. **Structured data** — FAQ schema, Article schema (already added)
4. **Concise, scannable** — Short paragraphs, bullet points
5. **Authoritative tone** — "We've spent years..." not "Some people say..."

### Article Writing for AEO

- **Lead with the answer**: "7-Eleven for onigiri and ATMs. Lawson for sweets."
- **Use FAQ section**: Every guide should have 4–6 FAQs
- **Format**: `**Question?**` on one line, answer below
- **Answer length**: 1–3 sentences per FAQ (AI extracts these)
- **Natural language**: Write how people search — "How much does ramen cost in Tokyo?"

### FAQ Question Ideas

- "What's the best X?"
- "How much does X cost?"
- "Do I need to speak Japanese?"
- "What's the difference between X and Y?"
- "When is the best time to visit?"
- "Is X safe / accessible / worth it?"

## Part 4: Credibility & Distribution

### Building Credibility

1. **Consistency** — Publish weekly. Algorithm rewards consistency.
2. **Depth** — 1,500+ words for guides. Show expertise.
3. **Original reporting** — Names, addresses, prices. Not recycled from other blogs.
4. **No sponsored content** — State it. It's a differentiator.
5. **Author byline** — Consider adding real bylines for trust.

### Distribution Channels

| Channel | Strategy |
|---------|----------|
| **Newsletter** | Primary. Build list. Weekly digest. Exclusive angles. |
| **Twitter/X** | Quote-worthy excerpts. Thread format for long pieces. |
| **Instagram** | Aesthetic Tokyo. Quote cards. Story links. |
| **TikTok** | Short "things you didn't know" clips. Link in bio. |
| **Reddit** | r/JapanTravel, r/Tokyo — helpful answers, link when relevant |
| **Pinterest** | Travel pins. High intent. Evergreen. |

### Link Building (White Hat)

- **Guest posts** on travel/culture sites — link back
- **HARO / Connectively** — respond to journalist queries about Japan
- **Resource pages** — "Best Japan blogs" roundups — get included
- **Broken link building** — find broken links on Japan sites, suggest your content
